## Frequently Asked Questions

### Licensing Questions

**Q: Can I use React Achievements in multiple projects?**
A: Yes, your commercial license covers unlimited projects within your organization.

**Q: What happens if I cancel my subscription?**
A: You must remove React Achievements from all commercial production deployments. Development and testing can continue.

**Q: Can I lock my dependency to a specific version and keep using it forever?**
A: No, the commercial license requires an active subscription for any commercial deployment, regardless of version.

**Q: Do I need separate licenses for development and production?**
A: No, one organizational license covers all environments (development, staging, production).

### Revenue and Tier Selection

**Q: We just started and have no revenue. Which tier should we choose?**
A: If you anticipate generating revenue, start with the Indie Developer tier.

**Q: Our revenue fluctuates around the tier boundaries. What should we do?**
A: Select the tier based on your annual revenue. If you exceed the limit, upgrade within 30 days.

**Q: We're a venture-funded startup with no revenue but large funding. Which tier?**
A: For venture-funded startups, consider the Professional tier as it better reflects your organization's size and resources.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Does the commercial license include custom development or features?**
A: No, but Enterprise tier sponsors can influence the roadmap. Custom development can be arranged separately.

**Q: What's included in "priority support"?**
A: Email support with guaranteed response times. Critical bugs are prioritized in development.

**Q: Can I get a refund if the library doesn't work for my use case?**
A: You can cancel anytime. Refunds follow GitHub Sponsors' policy. We recommend thorough testing before committing.

### Special Cases

**Q: I'm building an open-source project that also has a paid hosted version. What do I need?**
A: The open-source version can use the MIT license. The paid hosted version requires a commercial license.

**Q: We're a non-profit with a small revenue stream (e.g., donations). Do we need a license?**
A: Registered 501(c)(3) or equivalent non-profits can use the MIT license for free, regardless of donation revenue.

**Q: Can I include React Achievements in a product I sell to customers?**
A: Yes, if you have an active commercial license. Your customers don't need their own licenses for using your product.
